# How to Write a Resume That Passes Automated Screening Software

Did you know that 75% of resumes never reach a human eye? They are filtered out by **Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)**. If you're wondering **how to optimize my resume for applicant tracking systems (ATS)**, this guide is for you.

## Understanding ATS

An ATS scans your resume for specific keywords, formatting, and data structures. If your resume is too complex or lacks the right terms, it gets rejected.

## Top Tips for ATS Optimization

### 1. Simple Formatting is Key
Avoid using complex columns, graphics, or tables unless you are sure the parser can handle them. Stick to standard headings like "Experience", "Education", and "Skills".

### 2. Use the Right Keywords
Read the job description carefully. If they ask for "Project Management", don't just write "Managed projects". Use the exact terms.
